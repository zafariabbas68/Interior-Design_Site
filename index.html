Farm to Fork Project

Transforming transit spaces into vibrant community hubs through modular hydroponic farming and sustainable design

Ephemeral & Temporary Spaces

The "Farm to Fork" project reimagines the food chain by integrating on-site vertical farming with dynamic communal spaces. Utilizing hydroponic gardening, rainwater harvesting, and solar energy, this innovative solution brings fresh, plant-based food directly to campus communities while transforming unused walls into living, breathing ecosystems.

This project was developed as part of the "Ephemeral and Temporary Spaces" course at Politecnico di Milano, focusing on creating flexible, sustainable interventions that adapt to evolving campus needs while promoting environmental awareness and healthy living.

Academic Context

Politecnico di Milano | Ephemeral & Temporary Spaces Course

Timeline

2023-2024 Academic Year

Team

Group Project | 4 Team Members

Location

Bovisa Campus, Politecnico di Milano

01. Concept Development

Initial research and ideation phase focusing on sustainable food systems and campus space utilization.

  • Food production & procurement analysis
  • Campus transit space assessment
  • Sustainable materials research
  • Hydroponic system investigation

02. Design Strategy

Developing the modular system concept with scaffolding structures and adaptable units.

  • Modular scaffolding design
  • Unit typology development (Farming, Kitchen, Dining, Leisure)
  • Hydroponic integration planning
  • Material and structural specifications

03. Co-Design & Feedback

Engaging with campus community to gather insights and refine the design approach.

  • 86 participant survey and feedback collection
  • Plant-based diet concerns addressed
  • Space utilization preferences analyzed
  • Workshop and event integration planning

04. Technical Development

Detailed technical drawings, material specifications, and construction methodology.

  • Scale 1:100 and 1:50 technical drawings
  • Structural details and joint specifications
  • Material selection (polycarbonate, galvanized steel)
  • Hydroponic system integration details

05. Community Interaction

Public engagement through DIY hydroponic workshops and physical model demonstrations.

  • DIY hydroponic system workshops
  • 50+ recycled bottle conversions
  • Public awareness campaigns
  • Modular arrangement demonstrations

Lake Varese Landscape Project

Transforming lakefront spaces into sustainable ecological hubs through landscape design and community integration

Lake Varese Ecological Restoration

The Lake Varese Landscape Project focuses on ecological restoration and sustainable development of the lakefront area. This comprehensive design integrates natural ecosystems with recreational spaces, creating a harmonious balance between human activity and environmental preservation.

Developed as part of advanced landscape architecture studies, this project addresses critical issues of shoreline erosion, biodiversity conservation, and public access while creating visually stunning and functionally effective spaces for community engagement.

Academic Context

Landscape Architecture | Advanced Studio Project

Timeline

2023 Academic Year

Scope

Individual Comprehensive Project

Location

Lake Varese, Lombardy, Italy

01. Site Analysis & Research

Comprehensive analysis of ecological conditions, hydrological patterns, and existing site conditions.

  • Hydrological study and water quality analysis
  • Biodiversity assessment and habitat mapping
  • Soil erosion and shoreline stability analysis
  • Existing infrastructure evaluation

02. Ecological Design Strategy

Developing sustainable solutions for shoreline restoration and ecosystem enhancement.

  • Native vegetation planning and selection
  • Erosion control and shoreline stabilization
  • Wildlife habitat creation and enhancement
  • Sustainable drainage system design

03. Community Integration

Designing accessible public spaces that encourage community engagement with the natural environment.

  • Public access and circulation planning
  • Recreational facility design
  • Educational and interpretive elements
  • Universal accessibility standards

04. Technical Development

Detailed technical drawings, material specifications, and construction methodology.

  • Grading and earthwork plans
  • Planting plans and specifications
  • Hardscape and furniture details
  • Lighting and irrigation systems

05. Visualization & Presentation

Creating compelling visual representations using Twinmotion and other advanced visualization tools.

  • 3D modeling and realistic rendering
  • Animation and virtual walkthroughs
  • Presentation graphics and diagrams
  • Physical model construction

Academic Research

Exploring the intersection of spatial perception, light, and cultural heritage in contemporary interior architecture

Color Perception in Spatial Narratives: A Cross-Cultural Study

My research at Politecnico di Milano investigates how cultural background influences spatial perception through color and light manipulation in interior environments. This interdisciplinary study combines architectural theory with cognitive psychology.

Drawing from Persian architectural heritage and contemporary European design principles, the research develops a framework for creating culturally responsive spaces that enhance both aesthetic appeal and functional performance.

Methodology

Mixed-methods approach combining qualitative case studies with quantitative spatial analysis

Color Theory

Investigation of chromatic harmony in relation to spatial proportions and lighting conditions

Digital Modeling

Advanced 3D simulation of light behavior in complex spatial configurations

Cross-Cultural

Comparative analysis of Eastern and Western spatial design traditions
